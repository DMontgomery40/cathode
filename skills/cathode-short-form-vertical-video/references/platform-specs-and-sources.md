# Platform Specs And Source Ledger

Use this reference when platform format or policy detail matters. Re-check live sources for production work because platform specs and ranking guidance change.

## Cross-Platform Default

- Master as vertical 9:16, preferably 1080x1920, when the render pipeline supports it.
- Keep critical text, faces, logos, cursors, and CTAs inside a conservative centered safe zone.
- Burn in captions or concise on-screen text.
- Make audio useful, but make the short understandable without audio.
- Use native-feeling visual language: fast open, tight framing, text overlays, visible proof, and no landscape slide pacing.

## TikTok-Oriented Notes

- Start with the proposition in the first 3 seconds and prioritize the hook in the first 6 seconds.
- Use captions/text overlays for context.
- Use motion, transitions, graphics, stickers, creator-style framing, and sound to retain attention.
- Respect safe zones because UI overlays vary by placement and caption/add-on length.
- Avoid spam, fake engagement, and misleading clickbait.

## YouTube Shorts-Oriented Notes

- Shorts can be square or vertical and YouTube now supports Shorts up to 3 minutes.
- The first second matters; get to the story quickly.
- When adapting long-form, extract one standalone moment rather than summarizing the whole upload.
- Titles/thumbnails/metadata must accurately represent the video.

## Instagram Reels-Oriented Notes

- Use vertical 9:16 creative and design around Reels UI safe zones.
- Sound-on creative, voiceover, text overlays, stickers, effects, transitions, and human presence can make the piece feel native.
- Reels often tolerate a slightly more polished finish than TikTok, but the opening still needs immediate value.
